Leonhard Euler (1707-1783)

Leonhard Euler and Jean Bernoulli were important mathematicians. He wrote 800 different books and papers. Euler was born in Basel, Switzerland and later studied under Bernoulli.

Euler’s work Introductio in analysin infinitorum (1748) was a major influence on mathematics.

In the publication Lettres à une princesse d’Allemagne he wrote many explanations for mathematical ideas.

Euler used the symbol e in many equations. He also used the symbol π in his work.

The Most Beautiful Theorem?

The Most Beautiful Theorem?

Euler’s equation:

eix = cos(x) + i sin(x)

The expression also shows:

eiπ + 1 = 0

This uses the values e and i and also uses the symbol π again.

This formula is often discussed in the journal The Mathematical Intelligencer.
